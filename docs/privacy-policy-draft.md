# Candor Privacy Policy Draft

This draft is a starting point for legal review before publishing.

## Overview

Candor is a local-first desktop app for meeting transcription and note taking. The app is designed so your recordings, transcripts, notes, dictionary terms, settings, and calendar data stay on your device unless you choose to connect an external service.

## Information Candor Stores Locally

Candor may store the following on your computer:

- Your name and profile picture.
- App settings and theme preferences.
- Calendar connection metadata and credentials needed to sync calendar events. Credentials are stored through operating-system secret storage where available.
- Meeting recordings, if you choose to keep audio files.
- Transcripts, summaries, action items, dictionary terms, and notes.
- Local folder and storage library preferences.

## Calendar Connections

If you connect Microsoft Outlook, Google Calendar, or iCloud Calendar, Candor uses that connection to show your meetings inside the app. Calendar data is used to populate meeting context and is stored locally on your device.

## Audio and Transcription

Candor records audio only when you start recording. Transcription runs locally on your computer using Whisper technology. Audio is not sent to Candor servers for transcription.

## Optional External Services

If you connect a third-party AI service with your own API key in a future build, Candor may send selected meeting data to that service at your direction. Those services are governed by their own terms and privacy policies.

## Data Sharing

Candor does not sell your personal data. Candor does not operate a transcription server for your meetings.

## Data Deletion

You can delete recordings, transcripts, notes, profile information, and app settings from your local device. Uninstalling Candor may not delete notes stored in folders you selected.

## Contact

For alpha privacy questions, contact the maintainer who provided your build.
